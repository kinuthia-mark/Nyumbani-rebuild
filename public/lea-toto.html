Lea Toto Program

Bringing holistic HIV/AIDS care and hope directly to the heart of Nairobi's communities.

Nurturing the Child in the Community

Established in 1998, Lea Toto began as a response to the overwhelming number of HIV-positive children who could not be accommodated in residential homes. It shifted the focus from institutionalization to Community-Based Care (CBC).

Today, Lea Toto provides medical, nutritional, and psychosocial support to over 3,000 HIV-infected children and their families across eight centers in Nairobi's informal settlements.

Keeping Families Together

The core philosophy of Lea Toto is that a child thrives best within their own family. By empowering the community, we ensure that children remain in a familiar environment while receiving world-class medical attention.

  • Home-based care visits by trained community health workers.
  • Capacity building for local leaders and caregivers.
  • Prevention of Mother-to-Child Transmission (PMTCT) services.
