Who We Are

Nurturing hope and dignity for children affected by HIV/AIDS since 1992.

Our Vision

Sustainable communities for children infected and affected by the HIV pandemic inspired by Christian Compassion.

Our Mission

To provide quality comprehensive care and support to HIV infected and affected children, families and communities in a sustainable manner.

Our History & Legacy

Nyumbani (Swahili for "home") was founded in 1992 by Father Angelo D’Agostino, an American Jesuit and medical doctor, and Sister Mary Owens. At the height of the HIV/AIDS pandemic in Kenya, Father D’Agostino discovered that traditional orphanages were turning away HIV-positive infants.

Determined to provide a place of hope, he established the first hospice for HIV-positive children in Kenya. What started with three abandoned children has grown into a world-class institute serving over 4,000 children annually through four core programs.

A Holistic Approach to Care

We believe a child's well-being is a complete circle. Our care model seamlessly integrates medical treatment with nutritional support, quality education, psychosocial counseling, and spiritual guidance.

  • Child-Centered: Every action revolves around the individual needs of the child.
  • Sustainability: We empower families and communities to become self-reliant.
  • Dignity: We strive to eradicate the stigma associated with HIV/AIDS.

A Global Impact

Supported by international boards in the US, UK, Ireland, Spain, Italy, and Kenya, COGRI remains a recognized world leader in community-based solutions for HIV/AIDS.
