Nyumbani Village, Kitui

A pioneering, self-sustaining community providing a stable home for 1,000 orphans and 100 grandparents.

Restoring the Family Unit

The Village addresses the "lost generation" of Kenya by bringing together destitute grandparents and orphaned children to form new, blended families. Living in small cottage units, these families receive the love, culture, and stability needed to thrive.

Located on 1,000 acres in Kitui, the village is a haven for healing and growth, designed to be both bio-friendly and culturally respectful.

A Blueprint for Sustainability

Nyumbani Village is a global model for self-reliance. Through innovative green technology and sustainable agriculture, the village works toward total financial independence.

  • Trees for Children: Over 500 acres of Melia volkensii (hardwood) trees grown for sustainable timber revenue.
  • Smart Farming: Greenhouse agriculture, poultry, and dairy farming provide 100% of the village's fresh nutrition.
  • Renewable Energy: Extensive solar-power systems for the medical clinic and street lighting.
