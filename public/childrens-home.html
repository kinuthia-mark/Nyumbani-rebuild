Our History

Founded in 1992 by Father Angelo D‚ÄôAgostino and Sister Mary Owens, Nyumbani Children‚Äôs Home was Kenya's first hospice for HIV-positive orphans. At a time when other orphanages turned away HIV-infected infants, Fr. D'Agostino took matters into his own hands to provide a refuge of hope and dignity.

Today, the home has evolved from a hospice into a thriving residence for approximately 100 children, ranging from newborns to 23-year-olds.

Nyumbani is not just an institution; it is a village. Our children live in small family units designed to provide individual love and a sense of belonging.

Holistic Care & Services

Medical & Nutritional Excellence

We provide 24-hour clinical support, a full-service pharmacy, and strict adherence to ARV therapy. Each child receives a specialized nutritional plan to boost their immune system and ensure healthy growth.

Education & Life Skills

From primary schooling to vocational training in fashion design, carpentry, and hairdressing, we equip our youths to be self-reliant. Post-graduation career counseling ensures a successful reintegration into society.
